Beep
3D adventure game of a morphing alien
  • TASKS Conceptualization, illustration, sound design, 3d modelling, rigging, researching, coding, building
  • TOOLS Autodesk Maya, Unity3D, Clip Studio, Photoshop
  • DATE Spring 2017

Overview

Beep is a 3D game developed in Unity where user can control a spherical alien who can change itâ€™s shape to interact and solve puzzles in the environment. As player hides and dodges from hostile cube aliens in the different areas on the foreign planet, player will slowly uncover the hidden history of the planet.

In this project I am jumping between the role of concept artist, 3d modeller, and coder.

Concept Ideation

Beep at its very beginning in my sketchbook, initially allowed player to explore through three different planets and encounter different enemies on each one. In the conceptual stage, I worked mostly on environment, character, enemies, and color palette design. Ultimately due to time constraint, it was shortened to just one planet.

Coding

As our team has more designers than coder, I had to help our main programmers develop some minor features. These include exploring different camera option implementation, transparency shaders research, camera effects, minor UI implementation, particle effects, water planes, and sound implementation.

3D models & textures

My role in creating and texturing the models were focused on the design of the spaceship and the second enemy found in later level of the game. I explored different iteration of the spaceship model and enemy types in Maya. Textures were made in Photoshop and then implemented in Unity3D. While my team mates are perfectly capable in creating great textures for the models, I requested to texture the second enemy to learn the skill as well.
