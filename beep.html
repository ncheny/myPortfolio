Beep

3D game of a morphing alien
  • Beep is a Unity developed stealth/adventure game where user play as a morphing alien who crash landed on a foreign planet with hostile enemies and a hidden history.



  • TASKS Conceptualization, illustration, sound design, 3d modelling, rigging, researching, coding, building
  • TOOLS Autodesk Maya, Unity3D, Clip Studio, Photoshop
  • TEAM Danielle Ragas, Josephine Ryu, Wan-teen Yang
  • Spring 2017
Play the game Development Blog

Idea and Concept

Beep's concept started in my sketchbook as a 3D platformer where player traverse through three different planets with their own unique enemy types and boss. I designed a wide variety of enemies, boss types, enemy interactions, environment, character stages, enemies, color palette, and lighting direction concept art.

The fist world was designed to be comprised of cubes, the second world would have spikes, and the third world would be filled with triangles. Enemies in each world would reflect on the environment shapes. Similarly, player learns how to morph in the first world and would learn special attack skills as they move on to the next planet.

Coding

As our team has more designers than coder, I had to help our main programmers develop some minor features. These include exploring different camera option implementation, transparency shaders research, camera effects, minor UI implementation, particle effects, water planes, and sound implementation.

3D models & textures

My role in creating and texturing the models were focused on the design of the spaceship and the second enemy found in later level of the game. I explored different iteration of the spaceship model and enemy types in Maya. Textures were made in Photoshop and then implemented in Unity3D. While my team mates are perfectly capable in creating great textures for the models, I requested to texture the second enemy to learn the skill as well.
